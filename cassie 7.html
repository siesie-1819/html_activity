Champorado

Ingredients

  • 1 cup malagkit rice (or ½ malagkit + ½ regular rice)
  • 4 cups water
  • 4–6 pieces tablea (tsokolate)
  • ¼–½ cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: evaporated milk or condensed milk for topping

Steps

  1. Boil the rice: In a pot, combine rice and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat and stir occasionally so it won't stick.
  2. Add the tablea: When the rice is almost cooked and the mixture is starting to thicken, add the tablea and stir until the chocolate melts completely.
  3. Sweeten: Add sugar to taste and a pinch of salt to enhance the chocolate flavor. Taste and adjust sweetness.
  4. Simmer: Lower the heat and simmer until champorado becomes thick and creamy.
  5. Serve: Spoon into bowls and top with evaporated or condensed milk if desired. Traditionally served with dried salted fish (tuyo).

Tip: Use leftover rice for a slightly chewier texture, or soak the malagkit rice 15–30 minutes before cooking for faster cooking.
