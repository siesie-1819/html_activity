Pancit Canton

A flavorful Filipino stir-fried noodle dish served with vegetables, meat, and soy-based sauce.

Comparison to Other Recipes

Compared to Chinese chow mein which uses thinner noodles, Pancit Canton has a chewier texture and is seasoned with Filipino-style soy sauce and oyster sauce for a richer flavor.

Recipe Versions

Pancit Canton can be made with chicken, pork, shrimp, or a combination of all. Some versions are more saucy, while others are drier for easier serving.

Pancit Canton

A traditional Filipino noodle dish often served during birthdays and celebrations to symbolize long life.

Course Type: Main Dish

Cuisine: Filipino

Keyword: Pancit, Noodles, Stir-fry

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 25 minutes

Total Time: 45 minutes

Serving: 5

Calories: 420

Author: Cassie Corpin

Ingredients

  • 250g Pancit Canton (egg noodles)
  • 200g chicken breast, sliced
  • 100g shrimp, peeled
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cabbage, shredded
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Calamansi or lemon, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a wok. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken and shrimp. Cook until lightly browned.
  3. Stir in carrots, bell pepper, and cabbage. Cook until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add Pancit Canton noodles. Toss well until noodles absorb the sauce.
  6. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with calamansi.
